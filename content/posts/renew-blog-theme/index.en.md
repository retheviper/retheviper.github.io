This time, I decided to change the theme of my blog. The new theme is [NexT](https://github.com/Simpleyyt/jekyll-theme-next). Even though it's been a year, I'm not very familiar with Jekyll, so I'm having a harder time than I expected (I'm especially concerned about pages that disappear...), but I still think it has a much cleaner feel compared to the previous theme. It also had a search function, so I tried activating it, but it wasn't working locally, so I uploaded it to Github Pages and tested it. I want to use it as much as possible. (PS: Unfortunately, it seems like there was some kind of bug and I couldn't use it...)

It's been a year since I created this blog. Your first post was a year ago. At that time, I was a new employee who had just joined the company, so there were many things I didn't understand. Therefore, I think that the content of many of the posts I wrote were not so good. Lately, I've been doing a lot of development using Java, but I haven't had a very long experience with it, so when I look at the posts I wrote back then, I wonder why I wrote something as embarrassing as this.

Even so, I continue to update my blog because it is a record of my studies, and I am able to make new discoveries by looking back at the code and text I have written in the past. By looking back at past posts from time to time, I was able to find better and safer ways to do the same thing. There are quite a lot of things I would like to fix, but I think it would be a better way to track my career as an engineer if I wrote posts that would help me make up for it later.

It's a pretty embarrassing blog to show to anyone, but the number of people who visit it has increased. Like me, you're probably someone who has encountered some kind of problem or is searching for it out of curiosity to see what solutions others have found. I can't say that my own code or knowledge is correct, so I can't recommend that you use this blog as a reference, but as a fellow engineer, I hope you can take it as a result of my search for this kind of answer.

Well then, if you learn anything else, please let me know by post. Thank you for your continued support.
